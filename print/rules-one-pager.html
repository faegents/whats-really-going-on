What's Really Going On

A Conspiracy Theory Party Game  ·  3–10 Players  ·  Ages 16+  ·  30–75 min  ·  v2.3

The Decks

A — THE ACTORSWHO did it
B — THE ACTWHAT they did
C — EVIDENCEWHY / HOW
BOOSTChaos modifier

Setup

  • Shuffle each deck separately; place face-down.
  • Each player takes a Theory Sheet and pen.
  • First Theorist: whoever last said something conspiratorial. (Youngest if disputed.) Play clockwise.

Turn Phases

PhaseTimeWhat Happens
1 — DRAW — Draw 2 from each deck (A, B, C). Keep 1 from each. Unused cards → face-down to bottom of their deck. Chosen 3 → face-up center (replace previous round's cards).
2 — PLAN 60 sec Theorist preps. Panel: (a) secretly lock an Angle — MOTIVE / EVIDENCE / WITNESS (write actual Q after Presentation, not now); (b) agree which Shadow Evidence card to deploy.
3 — PRESENT 90 sec Deliver theory with conviction. Cover ACTOR + ACT + EVIDENCE. Over time = −1 pt.
4 — INTERROGATE 90 sec Draw 1 Boost Card. Panel reveals locked Angles simultaneously — Theorist sees what's coming. Panel writes first questions reactively. First Q per Panel member must match their Angle. Panel has 1 Shadow Evidence challenge. Theorist must answer; never say "I don't know."
5 — VOTE — Panel reveals tokens simultaneously. No self-votes.
6 — SCORE — Tally points. Theorist gives Best Question Award. Used theory cards stay face-up (Shadow Evidence for next round). Pass Theorist role clockwise.

Your Theory Formula

"[ACTOR] [ACT] because [EVIDENCE]."

e.g., "The Illuminati faked the moon landing because the Simpsons predicted it, down to the exact date."

Special Rules

  • ANGLE LOCK: During PLAN each Panel member secretly picks MOTIVE / EVIDENCE / WITNESS. Angles are revealed simultaneously at the start of Interrogation. Each Panel member's first question must use their locked angle — actual question written reactively after the Presentation.
  • SHADOW EVIDENCE: The 3 face-up cards = the previous Theorist's theory. During Interrogation the Panel has one challenge: pick 1 card and ask "If [that card] was the real [ACTOR / ACT / EVIDENCE], how does your theory survive?" No points — narrative weapon only. No challenge on round 1.
  • CITATION NEEDED: Panel member may call it once per Q&A. Theorist names a source (fake encouraged). Calling member alone decides — satisfying = +1 pt for Theorist.
  • Wild Cards: Each deck has 10. Name your own, combine two, escalate scale, or claim personal witness.
  • THE LEAK: Rare Boost card — Theorist secretly passes a written note to another player who reads it aloud as a genuine tip. +2 pts if it changed a vote.

10 Boost Cards (3 copies each)

  • DOUBLE DOWN — Add a secret controller
  • EXPERT WITNESS — Cite a fake expert by name
  • MEDIA COVERUP — Every answer starts "That's exactly what they want you to think"
  • CLASSIFIED FOOTAGE — Point to where evidence is hidden
  • TIMELINE SHIFT — Conspiracy started 200 years earlier
  • ALLY REVEALED — One Panel member is secretly in on it
  • IT'S BIGGER — Draw a 2nd ACT; add sub-conspiracy
  • THEY'RE IN THIS ROOM — Imply one questioner is an operative
  • ACCIDENTAL CONFESSION — Accidentally reveal something, then walk it back
  • NOTHING TO SEE HERE — Refuse exactly 1 question (explain why it's dangerous)

Optional Variants

Hot Take Mode No plan phase. 30s to choose cards, 60s present, 90s Q&A. Maximum chaos. Expert Mode No Wild cards. Play what you're dealt.
Deep State Ruling If theory accidentally makes sense (group consensus) → all others give that player 2 pts. Senate Hearing One player is THE SENATOR. Conducts all Q&A in formal congressional language. Best Question worth +2.
First to the Conviction Target wins  ·  You cannot say "I don't know"  ·  Laughing at your own theory is weakness
