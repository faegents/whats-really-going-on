What's Really Going On

A Conspiracy Theory Party Game for 2–6 Players
Ages 16+  |  45–90 Minutes
"They don't want you to play this game."

The world is run by shadowy forces. Everyone knows it. The question isn't whether a conspiracy exists — it's which one you've uncovered, and whether you can convince everyone else that yours is real.

In What's Really Going On, players draw from three decks to assemble a unique conspiracy theory, then present it to The Panel with total conviction. Survive hostile questioning, earn BELIEVER votes, and score points. The most persuasive theorist wins.

Object of the Game

Craft a conspiracy theory from your drawn cards, deliver it as if your life depends on it, and survive hostile questioning from The Panel. Earn BELIEVER votes to score points. First player to 20 Conviction Points wins.

Setup

  1. Shuffle each of the three main decks separately. Place them face-down in the center labeled A B C.
  2. Shuffle the Conspiracy Boost deck D separately. Place it face-down.
  3. Give each player a Theory Sheet and a pen.
  4. Determine the first Theorist — whoever most recently said something in real life that sounded like a conspiracy theory. If disputed, youngest player goes first.
  5. Play proceeds clockwise.

How to Play

Each turn belongs to one Theorist. All other players are The Panel.

Phase 1
DRAW

The Theorist draws:

  • 2 cards from Deck A (ACTORS)
  • 2 cards from Deck B (THE ACT)
  • 2 cards from Deck C (EVIDENCE)

Choose exactly one card from each deck to build your conspiracy. Discard unused cards face-down.

Your three chosen cards combine into one theory:

"[ACTOR] [ACT] [EVIDENCE]."

Examples:

  • "The Illuminati faked the moon landing because the Simpsons predicted it in 1998, down to the exact date."
  • "Lizard People created COVID-19 in a lab to harvest adrenochrome from the general public."
  • "NASA built a secret base on the dark side of the Moon to prepare Earth for the alien disclosure event scheduled for 2027."
Phase 2 — 2 Minutes
PLAN

The Theorist has 2 minutes of silence to prepare their presentation. Jot notes on your Theory Sheet. The Panel may talk quietly among themselves but not to the Theorist.

Phase 3 — 90 Seconds
PRESENT

The Theorist stands (encouraged) and delivers their conspiracy theory as if they genuinely believe it. The presentation must include:

  1. Name the ACTOR (who is behind it)
  2. State THE ACT (what they did)
  3. Explain the EVIDENCE — both the motive and the proof

When time is called, wrap up within one sentence. Going over forfeits 1 point.

Phase 4 — 2 Minutes
INTERROGATE

The Panel questions the Theorist. Rules:

  • Any Panel member may ask a question at any time
  • Questions must genuinely attempt to poke holes or demand elaboration
  • The Theorist must answer every question. They may not say "I don't know."
  • At the START of Q&A, draw one Boost Card D and read it aloud. Effect applies immediately.
  • CITATION NEEDED: Once per Q&A, a Panel member may call "CITATION NEEDED." The Theorist names a source (fake sources encouraged). Majority Panel approval = +1 CP (max 2 per turn).
Phase 5
VOTE

All Panel members simultaneously reveal one vote token:

BELIEVER — Convinced (or convinced enough) SKEPTIC — Not buying it, but solid effort PLANT — Government operative. Theory collapsed.

No discussion until after tokens are revealed. Cannot vote for yourself.

Phase 6
SCORE

Theorist earns:

ResultPoints
Each BELIEVER vote3 pts
Each SKEPTIC vote1 pt
Each PLANT vote0 pts
Unanimous BELIEVER+2 bonus pts
Majority PLANT−1 pt (floor: 0)
CITATION NEEDED answered well (max 2)+1 pt each
Boost Card fully executed+1 pt

Panel earns:

ResultPoints
Voted PLANT and majority also voted PLANT+1 pt
First to vote BELIEVER and Theorist wins the round+1 pt
Best Question Award (given by Theorist)+1 pt

Winning

First player to 20 Conviction Points wins.

Tiebreaker: tied players all present simultaneously with fresh draw; each non-tied player casts BELIEVER or PLANT. Most BELIEVERs wins.

The Conspiracy Boost Cards D

Drawn at start of Q&A. 30 total (3 copies each of 10 types).

NOTHING TO SEE HERE Refuse to answer exactly one question; explain why it's dangerous

THE LEAK Card

Shuffled into the Boost deck. When drawn, secretly pass it plus a written note to any player. That player reads the note aloud during Q&A as a genuine tip.

Wild Cards

Each deck has 4 Wild cards:

  • [YOUR CHOICE] ×2 — Name any actor/act/evidence of your choosing
  • [COMBO] — Play two cards of that type (both apply)
  • [INSIDER] / [ESCALATE] / [ONGOING] / [DOUBLE DOWN] / [PERSONAL WITNESS] — Deck-specific special effects

Optional Rules

Public Discard — One unused card placed face-up. Gives the Panel misleading intel.

The Deep State Ruling — If a theory accidentally makes factual sense, all other players award 2 CP in honor/horror.

Grand Unified Theory (5–6 players) — Spend 5 pts; use ALL recent discards. 3-min prep, 2-min present, 3-min Q&A. Scoring doubled. Failure = −7 pts.

Hot Take Mode — No planning phase. 30-sec card selection, 60-sec present, 90-sec Q&A.

The Senate Hearing — One player is THE SENATOR; runs all Q&A in formal congressional language.

Expert Mode — No Wild cards.

Cinematic Universe Mode — Must reference a previous round's theory as supporting evidence.
